# Example: Evaluating a Landing Page

## The Design (Description)

A product landing page with:
- Hero section: headline, subheadline, CTA button, hero image
- Features section: three feature cards in a row
- Social proof: logos of companies
- Pricing: three tiers in cards
- Footer: links and legal text

The page uses a blue accent color, 16px body text, 32px headline, and white background. All sections have 64px vertical spacing.

## Perceptual Evaluation

### Hierarchy: 3/5

The hero headline (32px, bold) dominates — correct. The CTA button uses the accent color — correct. But the three feature cards are all identical in size and weight, making them compete with each other rather than forming a clear progression.

**Finding:** Feature cards lack internal hierarchy. All three appear equally important.

**Fix:** If one feature is the primary selling point, make that card slightly larger or use a different background color. If all three are equal, ensure the reader knows they are options, not a sequence.

### Grouping: 4/5

Each section is clearly bounded. Cards group related content. The social proof section uses logo proximity to create a clear group.

**Finding:** Minor — the footer links are not grouped by category.

**Fix:** Group footer links under category headings (Product, Company, Legal).

### Rhythm: 4/5

64px spacing between sections is consistent. Card internal padding is uniform.

**Finding:** The gap between social proof and pricing is 64px, same as other sections. But the conceptual transition is larger (from "trust signals" to "buying decision").

**Fix:** Increase spacing between social proof and pricing to 96px to signal the transition.

### Emphasis: 3/5

The CTA button has strong emphasis (accent color, bold text). But there are two CTAs — hero and pricing — with equal treatment. The reader does not know which is primary.

**Fix:** Choose a primary CTA. De-emphasize the secondary one (outline style, muted color).

### Flow: 3/5

The hero → features → social proof → pricing flow is logical. But the reader must scroll through social proof (passive content) to reach pricing (active decision). The flow stalls.

**Fix:** Consider placing a summary CTA after social proof, before pricing, to maintain momentum.

### Balance: 4/5

The three-column layout is balanced. Hero section is centered. Footer is symmetrical.

**Finding:** The features section is left-aligned text with right-aligned image, creating slight left-heaviness.

**Fix:** Either center the features or alternate alignment (left, right, left) for visual rhythm.

### Completion: 4/5

Each section has clear beginning and end. The page feels resolved.

**Finding:** The footer feels abrupt. No visual transition from content to footer.

**Fix:** Add a subtle background color change or divider before the footer.

### Restraint: 4/5

Every element serves a purpose. No decorative elements.

**Finding:** The hero image is decorative — it does not communicate product value.

**Fix:** Replace with a product screenshot or remove if unnecessary.

### Total: 28/40 — Good. Production-ready with polish.

## Severity Classification

| Severity | Finding | Fix |
|----------|---------|-----|
| P2 | Feature cards lack internal hierarchy | Add primary card treatment |
| P2 | Two CTAs with equal emphasis | De-emphasize secondary CTA |
| P2 | Social proof → pricing flow stalls | Add summary CTA |
| P3 | Footer links not grouped | Add category headings |
| P3 | Footer feels abrupt | Add transition |
| P3 | Hero image is decorative | Replace or remove |
